The Gentleman's Hat Club.

Top Hat's

A tall, flat-crowned, broad-brimmed hat, worn by men from the latter part of the 18th to the middle of the 20th century.

Fedora's

A felt hat with a wide brim and indented crown, typically creased lengthwise down the crown and "pinched" near the front on both sides.

Fez's

A felt headdress of two types: either in the shape of a truncated cone, or a short cylinder, both usually with a tassel attached to the top.

Karakul's

A hat made from the fur of the Qaraqul breed of sheep. The hat is peaked and folds flat when taken off of the wearer's head.

Deerstalker's

A cap that is typically worn in rural areas, often for hunting, especially deer stalking, commonly associated with Sherlock Holmes.

Gatsby Cap's

A cap is that is rounder, fuller, and paneled with a button on top, and often with a button attaching the front to the brim.

Flat Cap's

A rounded cap with a small stiff brim in front, typically associated with older working class men.

Bowler's

A hard felt hat with a rounded crown, often called "the hat that won the West".


Balmoral's

A traditional Scottish hat, taking the form of a knitted, soft wool cap with a flat crown.
